## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters and hiring managers at software engineering, quant finance, and ML/AI companies reviewing this portfolio during internship hiring cycles. Secondary audience: technical peers (hackathon judges, professors). Viewed on desktop primarily during recruiting season. The visitor spends ~60 seconds deciding if they want to reach out.

### Brand Personality
Ambitious, technical, rigorous. Three words: **sharp / quantitative / earned**. Not trying to be "cool" — trying to signal competence and depth. The projects are genuinely impressive (3rd place DL Week, 2nd place HacX, Gold WorldQuant, NAISC Singtel finalist) and the design should reflect that weight.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme — appropriate for a technically-oriented audience viewing late at night during recruiting cycles. Amber/gold accent currently used; this has potential but is being underutilized. Should feel like a research paper or quant desk terminal crossed with editorial design — precise, data-dense, confident. Anti-reference: generic Tailwind portfolio with glowing cards. The visitor should feel they are reading serious work, not browsing a template.

### Design Principles
1. **Differentiate by substance** — the projects are varied (finance, ML, full-stack, systems). The design should reflect this variety, not flatten everything into identical cards.
2. **Signal credibility, not aspiration** — every design choice should reinforce that this person has shipped real things under real constraints.
3. **Information density over decoration** — a recruiter who wants more will read; one who wants less will skim. Serve both.
4. **Restraint as confidence** — adding more amber glow is not the answer. White space and precision communicate authority better than decorative flourishes.
5. **Honest hierarchy** — wins and serious work should stand out from coursework. Not everything deserves equal visual weight.

### Track System (new — April 2026)
Post-hero popup asks visitors which track they want to view. Selection both **filters content** (only relevant projects/sections shown) and **applies that track's visual identity** (color + background pattern). Popup appears on every visit.

**Four tracks:**
- **Quantitative Finance** — color `oklch(76% 0.155 65)` amber/gold. Content: quant projects, WorldQuant hackathon, quant skills.
- **Machine Learning & AI** — color `oklch(62% 0.11 158)` seafoam/teal. Content: ML projects, Sentinel/DL Week, ML skills.
- **Full Stack** — color `oklch(65% 0.14 260)` electric blue. Content: HomeCast, PRISM, FIN.IQ, SC2002, full-stack skills.
- **View Everything** — color `oklch(74% 0.04 65)` near-silver. Content: full portfolio, all categories visible.

**Popup UX:**
- Cycling flip-word display (split-flap/departure-board animation) showing the 4 track names
- Dropdown selector + "Go" button
- Popup background pattern shifts as user cycles through options
- On "Go": slot machine animation (3 reels) as loading screen, background transitions to selected track's pattern
- Reels stop, page loads filtered+themed, popup dismissed

**Each track has its own background pattern** (color + geometric texture) applied as a full-page texture overlay on `oklch(8.5% 0.006 65)` base. Patterns are CSS-only (no image assets), low opacity (1–4%), non-distracting.
